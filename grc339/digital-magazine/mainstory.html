Sitting Down with Tanya Ravichandran

In the fast-paced, ever-evolving world of fashion tech, where trends are born and die in a TikTok minute, one individual stands out as a true visionary. Tanya Ravichandran, at just 23, isn't merely collecting archival pieces; she's meticulously curating a movement, proving herself to be the undisputed queen of vintage fashion archive collecting for the digital generation.

Her discerning eye and profound knowledge boast treasures like Dior's F/W 2002 leather mirror pants – a nod to Rajasthani artisanry and a testament to her commitment to cultural inclusion. Prada sets from the late '90s, an Alexander McQueen "milkmaid" dress, and a Maison Martin Margiela sequin print Trompe L'Oeil Maxi Dress currently gracing the halls of The Met, all find a home in her collection And let's not forget the Yves Saint Laurent Tom Ford-era deer-print lace-up ensemble that looks tailor-made for her. This isn't just a collection; it's a living, breathing museum of fashion history, worn and celebrated.

Tanya forged her own way, starting as a precocious 10-year-old photographer on Instagram to now a well-recognized creative director and photographer. Her knowledge of fashion history is truly captivating. She can rattle off facts about Ann Demeulemeester with the passion of a seasoned professor, detailing everything from tags to the iconic Antwerp Six. She is not solely a collector, she is a true archivist, deeply invested in the narrative behind each garment.

But Tanya isn't about preserving these pieces behind velvet ropes. Her philosophy is refreshingly punk rock: "wear them to the ground because they look cooler that way." This ethos, reminiscent of Vivienne Westwood or Alexander McQueen's rebellious spirits, democratizes archival fashion, bringing it out of the elite estates and into the hands of a Gen Zer who fearlessly shares its beauty and history with her burgeoning online community.

She embodies the change she wishes to see, inspiring young South Asians to pursue creative careers and challenging the notion that talent isn't met with equal opportunity.

In a landscape dominated by fleeting trends, Tanya Ravichandran stands as a beacon of authenticity and depth. She's not just a collector; she's a storyteller, an educator, and a trailblazer. In the realm of fashion tech, where innovation meets heritage, Tanya Ravichandran has undeniably cemented her status as the reigning queen of vintage fashion archive collecting, proving that true style is timeless, and its stories deserve to be told, and worn, by all.
